Design Thinking · San Francisco Transit · Team Barefoot

The Problem

The Daily
Strain

"It's just… my day-to-day commute shouldn't feel like survival, you know?" Muni Rider Interview

How might we redesign an experience that gives commuters more comfort and control, without losing the civic spirit of public transportation?

Finding

Mood is lower during the commute than any other daily activity

Finding

Reliability, cleanliness, and crowding are the biggest satisfaction gaps

Experience Blueprint

What We
Heard

01

Comfort as Control

"I like the bus when it's empty; I can listen to music and just be calm." Riders don't want luxury. They want agency over how they feel during the ride.

02

Crowding Breeds Stress

"I don't know whether it's going to be a rowdy group of kids or an aggressive person." Overcrowding triggers anxiety and aggression, degrading the shared experience.

03

Uncertainty Creates Anxiety

"If there are too many people, it gets loud and stressful. I just wait for the next one." Not knowing seat availability forces riders to sacrifice time for peace of mind.

04

Equity Is Non-Negotiable

"Public transport should feel fair, not like some people get comfort and others don't." Any solution must preserve civic spirit, not create a two-tier underclass.

Current State of the Art

No Middle
Ground

Every current option trades off either comfort or equity. There's no middle ground between public accessibility and personal control.

✗ Limited coverage & last-mile access

Personal Transit

✓ Full control & privacy

✗ Costly, parking stress, traffic delays

Rideshare

✓ Convenient & on-demand

✗ Expensive, isolates riders

01 Regression Analysis

What Makes a Viewer Watch? Unpacking Pre-Roll Ad Engagement

Regression Modeling · 10,000 viewer records · South Korean Video Platform

Business Problem

A video platform needs to know which content genres and brand categories keep viewers from skipping ads. The goal is to inform smarter ad placement and give advertisers the data to justify premium pricing.

The data revealed that not all ad slots are equal. Where an ad plays and what brand it represents matters far more than most platforms account for. Running four models on 10,000 viewer records confirmed these effects hold up under rigorous testing.

Sports content drove the highest completion lift of 23.8pp. Price sports slots at a premium.
Finance ads outperformed every other category with a 41.3% completion rate.
PC Web users completed 31.6% of ads vs 8.9% on Mobile App. Device targeting is non-negotiable.

02 Factor Analysis

What Actually Drives Music Listening? A Factor Analysis of Consumer Motivation

Exploratory Factor Analysis · 27 Survey Items · Multiple Regression

Business Problem

A music platform wants to understand what psychologically drives listening behavior. Knowing the real motivations behind consumption is what makes content strategy and marketing campaigns actually land.

The data revealed three distinct motivational dimensions behind music listening. Not all of them drive consumption equally, and that distinction has real implications for how platforms and artists should market themselves.

Social identity is the strongest driver of listening time (β=1.77). People who share and discuss their music taste listen significantly more.
Emotional resonance does not predict listening volume — it shapes virality, not habit.
Invest in community and self-expression features. Curated playlists serve the background listener.

Who Are Your Cable Customers Really? A Segmentation Study for Smarter Marketing

K-Means Clustering · Video, Internet and Phone Spending Data

Business Problem

A cable operator was treating all subscribers the same. To grow engagement and long-term value, they needed to understand who their customers actually are and what distinct spending patterns each group has.

Six meaningfully different customer segments shaped primarily by age and income. A key decision was choosing not to standardize the data, which would have distorted the phone variable and masked real behavioral differences.

Six segments emerged. Family-Focused Heavy Watchers are the highest-value group with the largest households and highest income.
Phone service is weak across all six segments — a clear upsell opportunity.
Personalize pricing and content offers by segment rather than by service tier alone.

04 Predictive Modeling

Less is More or Less is a Bore? Device Behavior and Subscriber Churn

Logistic Regression + K-Means Clustering · 105,000+ Subscriber Records

Business Problem

A digital media publisher was losing subscribers and needed to understand which user behaviors predicted churn. Does reading across multiple devices actually build loyalty, or is it just a usage pattern?

Device behavior is a stronger churn signal than most publishers track. Multichannel readers are not just more engaged; they are meaningfully less likely to leave. That finding held up across both clustering and logistic regression.

Multichannel users churn at 0.65% vs 1.1% for single-channel. Broader device engagement builds real loyalty.
Mobile-only users are the highest churn risk at 1.54%. Tablet-primary multichannel users are the most loyal at 0.57%.
Prioritize cross-device features and use app adoption nudges for mobile-only users before they churn.
